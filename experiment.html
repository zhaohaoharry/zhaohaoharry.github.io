Classroom Experiments

DCA experiments and student access

The current experiment program compares three trading institutions in token reallocation environments: a uniform-price consignment auction, a continuous double auction, and a two-sided consignment auction with a clinching mechanism.

Student access

Live classroom links are posted here on experiment days. Students should open the current session link, log in with their subject ID and passcode, complete the introduction and quiz, and then wait for the session to begin.

Current experiment

DCA Experiments

Live classroom study

Participants receive an initial endowment, work through instructions and a quiz, and then trade through repeated rounds in a controlled laboratory session. The design lets us compare allocation, prices, and strategic behavior across institutions.

Treatments

Three market institutions

Treatment 1

Uniform-price consignment auction

Subjects first surrender their endowment to the mechanism. Tokens are then redistributed in an ascending-bid consignment auction with a uniform clearing price.

Treatment 2

Continuous double auction

Buyers and sellers can post bids and asks continuously, and they can also accept existing quotes, as in a standard stock-market style trading environment.

Treatment 3

Two-sided clinching mechanism

This treatment implements the project's own two-sided consignment auction with clinching, allowing us to compare it directly against the two benchmark institutions.
